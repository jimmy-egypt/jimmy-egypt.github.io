Negative space

Wed 23 September 2020
movement improv bodily awareness space

Intent: Exploring the movement of your body in space

Negative space is a concept widely used across the arts, referring to the empty space, the space that is not filled. When moving your body, you constantly define new spaces with your body parts. By having your legs apart, you create a space inbetween, just as by holding your arms up or tilting your head forwards. Recognising the negative space allows you to fill that space again. Keeping this in mind, everyone can do their own research, looking for spaces to define and to fill in again.
